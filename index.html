  • The Battles that Forged his Legacy

      528 AD

      Battle of Thannuris

      This was his first battle as Master of the Army. Him and four other commanders had to defend the fortress at Thannuris. This battle was a horrible defeat for Rome as two of their commanders fell and Belisarius and the other had to flee their remaining forces to the city of Dara. Although, it was a purric victory for the Persians, for they lost over 5000 Persian Immortals.

      530 AD

      Battle of Dara

      The most famous battle of his career. This was the battle where he the 10th Legion, the laughing stock of the Roman military, took down the Persian Army and protected the city of Dara. This was the battle that forced the world to look.

      531 AD

      Battle of Callinicum

      The most controversial battle Belisarius ever fought. Solely on the fact that it was the only battle he ever lost. This one lost is the reason some historians do not consider him one of the greatest military commanders.

      532 AD

      Nika Riots

      The riots that burned down Constantinople. The riotors were fearless until they saw Belisarius and his Bucellarii enter the Hippodrome. It dawned on the rioters that they were cornered and they had no where to run. They all were killed for their actions.

      533 AD

      Battle of Ad Decimum

      At this battle, he was able to defeat the Vandals of Carthage and return the city to the Roman people. This was the start of the end of the Vandal Kingdom.

      533 AD

      Battle of Tricamarum

      The battle took place twelve weeks after the Battle of Ad Decimum. It was King Gelimer's attemp at recapturing Carthage. Instead of a siege, Belisarius charged his calvery head on and wiped the Vandal army. Gelmimer fled to Spain, but knew his kingdom was doomed.

      535 AD

      Seige of Panormus

      The battle that was the begining of the Gothic Wars. Belisarius defeated the stronghold of Sicily and took all of Sicily for the Byzantine Empire.

      536 AD

      Seige of Naples

      Following Sicily, he launched his campaign against Naples and took the city by using a hidden entrance under the aquaduct.

      537-538 AD

      Seige of Rome

      One of the most famous battles of his career where he defended Rome from the Goths. The battle where he levied the citizens of Rome and defeated the Goths.

      538 AD

      Seige of Ariminum

      After the success of the Seige of Rome, the Byzantine military captured the forified city of Ariminum and further ruined the Goths.

      538 AD

      Seige of Urbinum

      The same result as the previous, half the battles of the Gothic wars weren't as exciting due to how more skilled Belisarius was compared to the Otsrogoths.

      539 AD

      Seige of Auximum

      Was able to be easily camptured like the Seige of Ariminum. With each victory, the Ostrogothic king was begining to see the reality of the situation.

      539-540 AD

      Seige of Ravenna

      Immediately after the Seige of Auximum, he set his sights on Ravenna, once the former campital of the Western Roman Empire. and conquered it as well.

      544 AD

      Seige of Otranto

      After the defeat of the Goths here, the Gothic king was becoming desperate at the situation, because he knew his kingdom was doomed.

      546 AD

      Sacking of Rome

      This was the battle that lost Rome to the Goths, but to little effect overall. In truth, this was more of a symbolic victory for the Goths.

      541 AD

      Seige of Sisauranon

      The sole battle Belisarius took part in during the Lazic War. Although he was victorious, he was needed elsewhear as the war with the Goths was becoming dire.

      559 AD

      Battle of Melantias

      The final battle of his career. Through his expert commanding, he was able to obliterate the enemy forces with barely any causalties on his side. Even though he won, it waws for nothing. After countless battle, he fell to the black plague 6 years later.
